PART A: CASE STUDY

UPI/Online Payment Fraud

What is the Crime?
UPI and online payment fraud involves deceiving individuals into authorizing fraudulent financial transfers or revealing sensitive banking credentials, such as UPI PINs or OTPs, over digital payment networks.

How It Happens (Step-by-Step)

1.Initial Contact: Scammers reach out via phone calls, SMS, or social media, frequently posing as bank officials, customer support reps, or interested buyers on online marketplaces.

2.The Manipulation: They create a sense of urgency (e.g., "Your account will be suspended") or confusion. A common tactic is sending a "Collect Request" on a UPI app while falsely claiming it is a link to receive a payment.

3.The Compromise: The victim is pressured into clicking a malicious link, scanning a fraudulent QR code, or entering their UPI PIN under the guise of verifying a deposit.

4.The Theft: Once the PIN or OTP is entered, funds are instantly siphoned from the victim's account and rapidly moved across multiple digital wallets or accounts to evade tracking.

Who is Targeted?
While anyone can be scammed, perpetrators primarily target senior citizens, individuals with limited digital financial literacy, and frequent users of online marketplaces who might be distracted or rushing through transactions.

The Consequences
Victims face immediate and often unrecoverable financial losses due to the instant nature of UPI. Beyond the monetary hit, this fraud causes severe emotional distress, a lingering loss of trust in digital banking systems, and potential vulnerability to further identity theft.
