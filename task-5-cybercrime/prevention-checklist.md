PART B: Prevention Checklist

Stay Safe Online: A Checklist for College Students in India

Navigating the digital world safely is essential, especially when balancing academic life, social media, and digital payments. Use this checklist to secure your digital footprint:

1.Secure Your UPI Transactions: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Fraudsters often send "Collect Requests" disguised as payments for items sold on marketplaces or for scholarship prizes. Remember: a PIN is only required to send money or check your balance.

2.Verify QR Codes: Avoid scanning QR codes sent via WhatsApp or Telegram to "verify" your identity or claim a reward. Only scan codes at trusted physical merchant outlets or verified official websites.

3.Use Student-Specific MFA: Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on your college portals, student email accounts, and social media. Using an authenticator app is significantly more secure than relying solely on SMS-based OTPs.

4.Protect Your Privacy on Campus Wi-Fi: Avoid accessing banking apps or sensitive student records over open college Wi-Fi. If necessary, use a reputable VPN to encrypt your connection and prevent "man-in-the-middle" attacks.

5.Audit App Permissions: Frequently review the permissions granted to apps downloaded for specific projects or coursework. Revoke access to your microphone, camera, and location for apps that do not strictly require them to function.

6.Identify "Urgent" Phishing: Be skeptical of emails or texts claiming your library access is suspended or your exam registration failed. Check the sender’s email address carefully; official university communications will typically come from a .ac.in or .edu.in domain.

7.Practice Password Hygiene: Use a dedicated password manager to generate unique, complex passwords for every platform. Avoid using the same password for your college login and your personal financial accounts.

8.Beware of Fake Job/Internship Offers: Scrutinize "Work from Home" offers received via social media that ask for a "security deposit" or "registration fee" for training materials. Legitimate companies will never ask you to pay for an internship or job.

How to Report Cyber Crime:

If you encounter a scam or experience financial loss, act immediately:

1.Call the National Helpline: Dial 1930 to report financial fraud and initiate the process of freezing stolen funds.

2.File a Formal Complaint: Visit the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal at cybercrime.gov.in to submit a digital report.
